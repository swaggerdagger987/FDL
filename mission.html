Open Terminal

MISSION

Build the Bloomberg Terminal for Dynasty Fantasy Football.

Fourth Down Labs exists to turn fragmented fantasy decisions into a coherent operating system: valuation, lineup impact, and opponent psychology in one workflow.

Product goal

One decision surface

Managers should not bounce across rankings, calculators, and forums. The terminal centralizes decision-critical context.

Portfolio-aware trade logic

Trade recommendations must account for starting lineup impact, downside risk, bye-week exposure, and roster construction.

Opponent intelligence moat

The long-term edge comes from multi-season behavior data: who overbids, who chases specific archetypes, and who negotiates aggressively.

Design principles

Clarity over noise

Every output must answer a real decision question: accept, reject, counter, or wait.

Evidence over vibes

Use concrete data (transactions, FAAB bids, roster churn, scoring settings) before narrative interpretation.

Speed with depth

Fast free-tier results drive adoption; deeper pro workflows handle high-leverage moves.

Single-founder operability

Architecture choices prioritize reliability and iteration velocity over complexity theater.
